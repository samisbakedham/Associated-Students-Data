Documented

Built from meeting records, not gossip.

Method

Sharp about accountability. Careful about proof.

What this project does

It organizes the Senate record into a public-facing map of meetings, votes, role changes, funding decisions, and governance failures so students can follow patterns instead of isolated moments.

What this project does not do

It does not label people corrupt without evidence. The archive shows serious process failures, opaque finance, and internal power struggles. Those are documented facts. Criminal conclusions require evidence outside these PDFs.

Why this matters

Student fees, honoraria, committee appointments, and public accountability all run through these systems. When the systems are weak, students lose visibility into how power is exercised on their behalf.
